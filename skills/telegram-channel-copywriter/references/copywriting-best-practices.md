# Copywriting best practices (web-grounded)

## 1) Write for scanning, not linear reading
- Assume people scan first, read second.
- Use meaningful subheads, bullets, one idea per paragraph, inverted pyramid.
- Cut word count aggressively; concise + scannable + objective copy improves usability.

Why: NNGroup reports users mostly scan web content; concise/scannable/objective writing improved measured usability significantly in controlled tests.

## 2) Lead with a hook and keep one promise
- Open with tension, sharp question, contrarian claim, or concrete problem.
- Deliver on that hook quickly; avoid bait-and-switch.

Why: Social copy guidance (Sprout) emphasizes hook-first structure and audience-specific framing.

## 3) Standardize winning templates
- Turn high-performing post structures into reusable templates.
- Reuse proven skeletons; vary angle/examples.

Why: Template reuse reduces production friction and stabilizes outcomes (Sprout).

## 4) Repurpose, don’t reinvent
- Convert one long asset into multiple short posts.
- Extract quote, summary, checklist, and anti-pattern from one source.

Why: Repackaging prior successful material is a core social efficiency pattern (Sprout).

## 5) Use active voice and objective language
- Prefer: subject -> verb -> object.
- Remove vague claims and inflated adjectives without evidence.

Why: Objective language lowers cognitive load and increases trust (NNGroup findings).

## 6) Make copy accessible by default
- Short sentences, plain language, minimal jargon.
- CamelCase hashtags, avoid ALL CAPS, avoid cluttered symbols.

Why: Accessibility recommendations from social practice guidance (Sprout).

## 7) Use one CTA per post and test variants
- Choose one CTA goal: comment, share, click, save, or reply.
- A/B test CTA wording over batches of posts.

Why: CTA quality strongly affects engagement/conversion in social writing practice (Sprout + copywriting frameworks).

## 8) Respect Telegram delivery constraints
- Message text limit is finite (use concise formatting).
- Keep formatting robust (HTML/Markdown as needed).
- Avoid dense walls of text; break into bullets/short paragraphs.

Why: Telegram Bot API method constraints and formatting rules.
